AI Reshapes the Global Economy
as Nations Race to Regulate
Experts warn of sweeping disruption to labour markets as governments convene emergency summit in Brussels
By Kweku Mensah, Technology Editor  •  June 6, 2026
Markets & Labour

Across the globe's largest financial centres, artificial intelligence is rewriting the rules of production at a pace that has left economists and policymakers scrambling to keep pace with events on the ground.

The International Monetary Fund, in a report released Thursday, estimated that up to 40 percent of jobs in advanced economies are exposed to AI automation — a figure that rises sharply in knowledge-intensive sectors.

"The question is no longer if machines will think, but whether we are ready."

Central banks have begun stress-testing their projections against rapid displacement scenarios, while labour unions in fourteen countries have called for immediate legislative protection for workers.

[ Photograph ]
Government Response

Governments from Washington to Berlin have pledged accelerated regulatory frameworks, but critics argue that such promises arrive years behind the deployment curve of the technology they seek to govern.

The EU's AI Act, which came into partial effect earlier this year, has been described by industry leaders as well-intentioned but ill-equipped for the speed of frontier model deployment.

In the United States, a bipartisan Senate committee concluded a six-month inquiry this week producing 180 pages of findings but no binding legislation, with members acknowledging the difficulty of writing law for a technology that evolves faster than the legislative calendar.

Hundreds Face Displacement

On factory floors from Shenzhen to Stuttgart, the human cost is already measurable. Three major automotive manufacturers announced this week a combined reduction of 48,000 positions, with AI-driven assembly cited in each case.

Workers in the knowledge economy, once insulated from automation, now find themselves on uncertain ground. Paralegals, junior analysts, medical coders, and writers have each seen platform adoption accelerate dramatically over the past eighteen months.

"We built careers on skills that took years. Now the same output arrives in seconds."

The Brussels summit, opening Monday, is expected to produce a communiqué committing states to coordinate on AI employment policy, though substantive enforcement mechanisms remain elusive.
